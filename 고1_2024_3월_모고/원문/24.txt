Working around the whole painting, rather than 
concentrating on one area at a time, will mean you can 
stop at any point and the painting can be considered 
“finished.” 
Artists often find it difficult to know when to stop 
painting, and it can be tempting to keep on adding more 
to your work. 
It is important to take a few steps back from the painting 
from time to time to assess your progress. 
Putting too much into a painting can spoil its impact and 
leave it looking overworked. 
If you find yourself struggling to decide whether you have 
finished, take a break and come back to it later with fresh 
eyes. 
Then you can decide whether any areas of your painting 
would benefit from further refinement.
