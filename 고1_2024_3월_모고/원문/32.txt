No respectable boss would say, ”I make it a point to 
discourage my staff from speaking up, and I maintain a 
culture that prevents disagreeing viewpoints from ever 
getting aired.“ 
If anything, most bosses even say that they are pro­dissent.
This idea can be found throughout the series of conversations
with corporate, university, and nonprofit leaders, published 
weekly in the business sections of newspapers. 
In the interviews, the featured leaders are asked about their 
management techniques, and regularly claim to continually 
encourage internal protest from more junior staffers. 
As Bot Pittman remarked in one of these conversations: ”I 
want us to listen to these dissenters because they may 
intend to tell you why we can’t do something, but if you 
listen hard, what they’re really telling you is what you 
must do to get something done.“
