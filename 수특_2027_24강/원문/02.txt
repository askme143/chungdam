Although many U.S. Americans consider mathematics boring and
tedious, all forms of standardized rankings involve figures and
numbers. When ranking quarterbacks, for instance, the NFL
(National Football League) standardizes the ranking process by
defining different numerical categories that cover all aspects of
the position. Each quarterback has certain numbers and figures,
ranging from “percentage of pass completions” to “interceptions
over touchdowns” to “number of yards passed per game.”
The quarterback’s livelihood depends on those numbers.
To negotiate a raise, a quarterback has to improve his numbers,
and when a quarterback is benched, it is often due to
unsatisfactory numbers. Similarly, at least 13 million Americans
belong to virtual football leagues in which participants select
players from different professional teams; at the end of the
season, the participant whose virtual team has amassed the
most points, defined by each virtual league differently, wins the
jackpot. Such leagues even have a draft at the beginning of
the season.
