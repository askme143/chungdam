project investigated the differences between people who found
their lives to be happy and those who found them to be
meaningful. While, on average, happy lives and meaningful
lives overlap a great deal, the researchers focused on those
aspects of a meaningful life that were distinct from happiness.
 And, remarkably, they found that people who had more
meaningful lives reported that they had more experience
suffering through past negative events. They also reported
worrying more and enduring more things that caused them stress.
 On the other hand, these past experiences with suffering tended
to reduce people’s overall levels of happiness.
