interpretation or definition of a given situation becomes a
subjective reality from that person’s viewpoint. We often
assume that what we consider to be “reality” is shared by
others; however, this assumption is often incorrect.
 Subjective reality is acquired and shared through agreed-upon
symbols, especially language.
Symbols are instrumental in helping people derive meanings If a person shouts “Fire!” in a crowded movie theater,
for example, that language produces the same response
(attempting to escape) in all of those who hear and understand
it. When people in a group do not share the same meaning
for a given symbol, however, confusion results; for example,
people who did not know the meaning of the word fire would not
know what the commotion was about. How people
interpret the messages they receive and the situations they
encounter becomes their subjective reality and may strongly
influence their behavior.
