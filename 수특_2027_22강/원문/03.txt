 They also spread seeds through their eating and
defecating habits and maintain or create water holes by
wallowing. All these roles are beneficial to other
species. Conversely, where human interference prevents elephants from
moving freely within their habitats and leads to their numbers
exceeding the carrying capacity of the savanna, their effect can
be environmentally disastrous.
