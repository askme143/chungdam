We are exceptionally smart, and this helps us adapt to a
wide range of environments.
But we are not nearly smart enough as individuals to
solve the adaptive problems that confronted modern
humans as they spread across the globe.
The package of tools, foraging techniques, ecological
knowledge, and social arrangements used by any group of
foragers is far too complex for any individual to create.
We are able to learn all the things we need to know in
each of the many different environments in which we live
only because we acquire information from others.
We are much better at learning from others than other
species are, and we are motivated to learn from others
even when we do not understand why our models are
doing what they are doing.
This psychology allows human populations to accumulate
pools of adaptive information that greatly exceed the
inventive capacities of individuals.
