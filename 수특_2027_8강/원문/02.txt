The graph above shows how employed adults viewed the
impact of AI adoption on their job opportunities by income level
in 2024. Among all employed adults, those expecting fewer
job opportunities from AI outnumbered those expecting more,
while nearly a third foresaw little impact and fewer than 20%
remained unaware of workplace AI use. More than one-third
of lower-income workers and about one-third of
middle-income workers said AI would lead to fewer job
opportunities, compared to about one-fourth of upper-income
workers. Over 40% of upper-income workers said AI would
not make much difference, which was more than twice the share
of middle-income workers who said the same.
