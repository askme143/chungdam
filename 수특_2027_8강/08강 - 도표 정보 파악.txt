The graph above shows the percentages of U.S. teenagers who
spent time with friends by communication type, based on a
survey conducted between 2014 and 2015. In the category of
Every Day, text messaging showed the highest percentage among
the four types of communication that teenagers used to spend
time with friends. In the category of Less Often, the
percentage of teenagers who spent time with friends through
talking on the phone was more than twice that of teenagers who
did so through text messaging. The percentage of teenagers
who spent time with friends through talking on the phone in the
category of Every Day was lower than that of teenagers who did
so in the category of Less Often. In the category of Less
Often, emailing was the second highest in percentage among the
types of communication listed above. The percentage of
teenagers who spent time with friends through video chatting in
the category of Less Often was higher than that of teenagers who
did so in the category of Every Day.
