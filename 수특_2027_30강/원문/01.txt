Not too much or too little — that’s what moderation means.
Moderation does not mean that you have to eliminate
low-nutrient-density foods from your diet, such as soft drinks
and candy, but rather that you can include them occasionally.
Moderation entails not taking anything to extremes.
You probably have heard that vitamin C has positive effects,
but that doesn’t mean huge doses of this essential nutrient
are appropriate for you.
It’s also important to remember that substances that are
healthful in small amounts can sometimes be dangerous in
large quantities. For example, the body needs zinc for
hundreds of chemical reactions, including those that support
normal growth, development, and immune function.
Too much zinc, however, can cause deficiency of copper,
another essential mineral, which can lead to damaged
immune function. Being moderate in your diet means that
you do not restrict or completely eliminate any one type of
food, but rather that all types of food can fit into a healthful
diet.
